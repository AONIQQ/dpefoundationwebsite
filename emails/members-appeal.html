Keep the doors of foreign service open

Dear Brother,

You know what Delta Phi Epsilon meant at Georgetown — a brotherhood built around service to country and a curiosity about the wider world. The Delta Phi Epsilon Foundation for Foreign Service Education carries that purpose forward, helping educate the next generation of American global statesmen.

Founded in 1962 and run entirely by volunteers, the Foundation awards scholarships to Georgetown students sitting for the Foreign Service Officer Test and pursuing careers in diplomacy and global business, recognizes service and innovation through our STIA Awards, and is reviving the lecture series that once brought leading practitioners to campus. It is the Alpha Chapter’s legacy, still at work.

Your gift directly supports that work:

$250 Sends a Georgetown student to the Foreign Service Officer Test (Bleakley Scholarship).
 
$300 Recognizes service and innovation through a STIA Award, with Georgetown’s School of Foreign Service.
 
$500 Backs a future global leader (Stanley Weiss or Halleck A. Butts Scholarship).
 
$1,000 Helps revive the lecture series and expand student programming.
Support the Next Generation →

A gift of any size honors what the fraternity gave you — and opens the same doors for a student who will carry the work forward.
