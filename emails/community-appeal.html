Help open doors to foreign service

Dear Friend,

Whether you have followed the Delta Phi Epsilon Foundation for Foreign Service Education for years or are just discovering us, thank you for your interest. For more than sixty years, our all-volunteer Foundation has worked to advance the study and practice of foreign service, helping educate the next generation of American global leaders.

Founded in 1962 and rooted at Georgetown’s School of Foreign Service, the Foundation awards scholarships to students sitting for the Foreign Service Officer Test and pursuing careers in diplomacy and global business, recognizes service and innovation through our STIA Awards, and is reviving a lecture series that brings leading practitioners to campus. Because the work is run entirely by volunteers, support goes a remarkably long way.

We are inviting friends like you to help us do more. Your gift directly supports students and programs:

$250 Sends a Georgetown student to the Foreign Service Officer Test (Bleakley Scholarship).
 
$300 Recognizes service and innovation through a STIA Award, with Georgetown’s School of Foreign Service.
 
$500 Backs a future global leader (Stanley Weiss or Halleck A. Butts Scholarship).
 
$1,000 Helps revive the lecture series and expand student programming.
